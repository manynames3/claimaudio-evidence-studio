Recommended length: 7-10 minutes when read naturally.

Demo targets:
- Liability: insured admits looking at navigation before braking.
- Damages: low-speed impact, no airbags, claimant vehicle drove away.
- Injury: claimant said he was fine at scene but later alleged neck/back injury.
- Prior Condition: insured mentions claimant was wearing a back brace or moving stiffly before collision.
- Coverage: insured was driving for a rideshare app but says app was off.

- Follow-up: app status logs, dashcam, repair estimate, scene photos.

Adjuster: This is Rachel Morgan with North Harbor Mutual. Today is May 17, 2026. This is a recorded statement for claim AUTO-BI-SYNTH-202. I am speaking with our insured, Evan Brooks. Evan, do I have your permission to record this statement?
Insured: Yes, you do.
Adjuster: Please confirm your name and address.
Insured: Evan Brooks. I live at 127 Pine Ridge Drive, Orlando, Florida.
Adjuster: We are discussing the May 15, 2026 accident involving your blue Honda Accord and a black Nissan Altima. Were you driving the Accord?
Insured: Yes.
Adjuster: Please tell me what happened.
Insured: Traffic slowed suddenly near the exit ramp. I braked, but the Altima in front of me stopped faster than I expected, and my front bumper touched the back of their car.
Adjuster: When you say touched, was there an impact?
Insured: Yes, but it was very light.
Adjuster: Were you stopped before contact?
Insured: I was basically stopped.
Adjuster: Basically stopped meaning completely stopped or still moving?
Insured: I may have been rolling, maybe five to ten miles per hour, but not fast.
Adjuster: About how fast were you traveling before traffic slowed?
Insured: Maybe twenty-five.
Adjuster: Earlier in your online report, you wrote forty miles per hour. Which is more accurate?
Insured: I meant the speed limit was forty. I do not think I was actually going forty.
Adjuster: What were you doing just before traffic slowed?
Insured: Watching traffic.
Adjuster: Were you looking at anything else?
Insured: My navigation screen was on. I glanced at it to check the exit, but I was not on my phone.
Adjuster: Was your phone mounted?
Insured: It was in the center console, connected to the car screen.
Adjuster: Was this a personal trip?
Insured: Yes, personal.
Adjuster: Do you drive for any rideshare or delivery company?
Insured: Sometimes, yes.
Adjuster: Were you logged into a rideshare app at the time?
Insured: No, the app was off.
Adjuster: Had you been working rideshare earlier that day?
Insured: I had just dropped someone off about ten minutes earlier, but I was done.
Adjuster: Were you waiting for another ride request?
Insured: I was not actively taking one. The app might have still been open, but I was not on a ride.
Adjuster: Did airbags deploy?
Insured: No.
Adjuster: Was your vehicle drivable?
Insured: Yes. I drove away after exchanging information.
Adjuster: What damage did you see?
Insured: At first I thought there was no damage. Later I saw the front bumper cover cracked on the right side.
Adjuster: Did the claimant's vehicle drive away?
Insured: Yes, the Altima drove away.
Adjuster: Did the claimant say he was injured?
Insured: No. He said he was fine. He was walking around and taking pictures.
Adjuster: Did you notice anything unusual about him physically?
Insured: He seemed stiff, but he also had a black back brace or support belt on under his shirt. I saw it when he bent down near the bumper.
Adjuster: Did police respond?
Insured: No, we exchanged information and left.
Adjuster: Were there witnesses?
Insured: I do not think so.
Adjuster: Do you have photos?
Insured: Yes, I took photos of both bumpers.
Adjuster: Do you have dashcam footage?
Insured: My dashcam might have recorded it, but I need to check the memory card.
Adjuster: Did you say anything to the claimant at the scene?
Insured: I apologized and said I did not expect him to stop that suddenly.
Adjuster: Do you believe you caused the accident?
Insured: I understand I hit him from behind, but I think he braked very suddenly.
Adjuster: Have you received a repair estimate?
Insured: Not yet. I have an appointment Monday.
Adjuster: Is there anything else we should know?
Insured: Only that it was a very minor bump. I was surprised when I heard he was claiming neck and back injuries.
Adjuster: Thank you. This concludes the recorded statement at 10:18 AM on May 17, 2026.
